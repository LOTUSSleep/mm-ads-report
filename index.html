Root Cause Analysis

Why 2026 Is Underperforming

It's not one thing. Five problems are compounding on each other and making every dollar work less.

Root Cause #1

Conversion Tracking Died

Smart Bidding used 2025 conversion data to find the right people at the right price. Without tracking, Google's algorithm is guessing who to show ads to. This alone explains the CPC jump from $0.37 to $1.29.

37,995 conv/mo→8,998 reported (broken)
Root Cause #2

PMax Lost Its Signal

PMax drove 46M of our 48M impressions in 2025 at near-zero CPC. Without conversion data feeding it, PMax can't optimize. It's now burning budget on low-quality placements instead of finding buyers.

4.02M imp/mo→2.22M imp/mo (−45%)
Root Cause #3

Competitor CPC Inflation

Endy search interest is up +12%. Sleep Country is bidding more aggressively online. Industry-wide, mattress CPCs have risen 15-25% in Canada. Our $0.37 average was unsustainably low and market forces are correcting it.

Root Cause #4

Landing Pages Hurt Quality Score

Size-specific keywords (king, queen, twin) land on a generic "all mattresses" page. Google sees poor relevance and charges more per click. Each point of Quality Score lost can raise CPC by 16-50%.

Root Cause #5

Budget Spread Too Thin

24 campaigns splitting $24K = $1,000/campaign avg. 5 campaigns are budget-limited. Yorkton and Moose Jaw Pillows get near-zero impressions. Small markets are starved while big campaigns waste on broad PMax.

The Fix

Everything Is Solvable

Fix tracking → Smart Bidding re-learns → CPCs drop. Build proper landing pages → Quality Score rises → CPCs drop further. Consolidate campaigns → budget flows to winners. 3-6 months to recover 2025 efficiency.

Staff Concern

Are We Showing for "Mattress Toppers" & "Air Mattresses"?

What We Found

We are NOT directly bidding on "mattress topper" or "air mattress" keywords. These terms are not in any of our 797 active keywords.

However, our Performance Max campaigns and broad match keywords can trigger ads on related searches. When someone searches "mattress topper saskatoon," Google may show our PMax ad because it contains the word "mattress."

Why This Matters

Staff are seeing customers come in expecting products we don't carry. This wastes ad spend and creates a poor customer experience. We're paying for clicks from people who want mattress toppers or air mattresses — products we don't sell.

Immediate Fix: Add Negative Keywords

Add these as account-level negative keywords today:

mattress topper air mattress inflatable mattress mattress pad camping mattress air bed crib mattress futon mattress cover used mattress second hand mattress recycling mattress disposal free mattress mattress protector waterproof mattress dog bed pet mattress

This blocks these searches across ALL campaigns instantly.
